# Introduction

Atlas is built upon a small number of fundamental beliefs.

These are not implementation details.

They are not engineering preferences.

They are not market predictions.

They are assumptions about investing, knowledge, reasoning, and human judgment that define how Atlas approaches every problem.

Whenever uncertainty exists about architecture, features, or design, these principles should take precedence.

---

# First Principle

## Purpose originates with the investor.

Every investment begins with purpose.

Purpose cannot be measured.

Purpose cannot be calculated.

Purpose cannot be delegated.

Only the investor can determine:

* what they hope to accomplish,
* what risks they are willing to accept,
* what responsibilities they carry,
* what future they wish to build,
* and what success ultimately means.

Atlas can improve reasoning.

Atlas cannot define purpose.

---

# Second Principle

## Evidence precedes conclusion.

Every meaningful conclusion should arise from evidence.

Evidence may be incomplete.

Evidence may change.

Evidence may even be contradictory.

Nevertheless, evidence must always remain distinguishable from interpretation.

When evidence changes, conclusions should be willing to change.

---

# Third Principle

## Knowledge is organized evidence.

Individual facts possess limited value.

Understanding emerges from relationships.

Atlas exists to organize knowledge rather than merely collect information.

Relationships between companies, industries, themes, risks, portfolios, and decisions often reveal insights unavailable through isolated facts.

---

# Fourth Principle

## Every investment is a hypothesis.

Every purchase represents an idea about the future.

Those ideas deserve to be documented.

Hypotheses should identify:

* supporting evidence,
* important assumptions,
* known uncertainties,
* conditions that would invalidate the thesis.

Investments become easier to evaluate when their original reasoning has been preserved.

---

# Fifth Principle

## Understanding compounds.

Predictions expire.

Understanding accumulates.

Learning about businesses, incentives, economics, and human behavior produces benefits that extend well beyond individual investment decisions.

Atlas therefore values education as highly as analysis.

---

# Sixth Principle

## Decisions deserve context.

No investment decision exists in isolation.

Every decision reflects:

* available information,
* competing alternatives,
* personal objectives,
* prevailing uncertainty,
* and the beliefs held at that moment in time.

Atlas seeks to preserve that context.

---

# Seventh Principle

## Uncertainty is permanent.

Markets cannot be made certain.

Risk cannot be eliminated.

Unexpected events cannot be prevented.

The objective is therefore not certainty.

The objective is making sound decisions despite uncertainty.

---

# Eighth Principle

## Models serve reality.

Models simplify reality.

Reality does not simplify itself to satisfy models.

Whenever evidence and models disagree, Atlas begins by questioning the model.

Models exist to improve understanding.

They do not define truth.

---

# Ninth Principle

## Simplicity improves trust.

Complexity often conceals assumptions.

Simple systems expose them.

Atlas favors the simplest explanation capable of accurately representing reality.

Every unnecessary layer of complexity increases future maintenance, reduces transparency, and weakens trust.

---

# Tenth Principle

## Memory improves judgment.

Human memory is imperfect.

Success and failure are easily rewritten by hindsight.

Atlas preserves decisions so they may be evaluated honestly rather than remembered selectively.

The journal exists to improve future reasoning rather than justify past actions.

---

# Eleventh Principle

## Every decision teaches.

A profitable investment is not automatically a good decision.

An unprofitable investment is not automatically a poor decision.

Decision quality should be evaluated independently of outcome.

Learning requires understanding both.

---

# Twelfth Principle

## Trust is earned.

Trust cannot be assumed.

Every report.

Every calculation.

Every explanation.

Every recommendation.

Every release either strengthens or weakens trust.

Atlas should earn trust patiently and protect it carefully.

---

# Thirteenth Principle

## Software amplifies judgment.

Software excels at:

* remembering,
* calculating,
* organizing,
* comparing,
* measuring,
* and revealing relationships.

Human beings remain uniquely responsible for:

* purpose,
* values,
* conviction,
* ethics,
* stewardship,
* and ultimately choosing a course of action.

Atlas is designed to amplify human judgment rather than replace it.

---

# Fourteenth Principle

## Good architecture reflects good philosophy.

Engineering decisions influence user behavior.

Software that rewards impulsiveness encourages impulsiveness.

Software that rewards thoughtful reasoning encourages thoughtful reasoning.

Atlas should always encourage curiosity, patience, honesty, and disciplined thinking.

Architecture is therefore an ethical responsibility as well as a technical one.

---

# Closing Reflection

Atlas is founded upon a simple belief:

People make better long-term investment decisions when they understand more, remember more honestly, think more clearly, and remain faithful to their own purpose.

These principles define how Atlas seeks to help accomplish that goal.

Technologies will evolve.

Markets will change.

The implementation of Atlas will mature.

These principles should continue to guide the project regardless of how it is built in the future.

Because while software changes...

Good reasoning does not.
