# CONSTITUTION.md

**Project Atlas**

**Foundation Document FDS-004**

**Status:** Draft for Founder Review
**Version:** 1.0-DRAFT
**Authority:** The Constitution is the highest governing document of Project Atlas. All architectural decisions, implementation choices, documentation, and contributions shall remain consistent with this Constitution unless it is formally amended.

---

# Preamble

Atlas was founded upon the belief that thoughtful investors deserve software that promotes evidence, transparency, privacy, and disciplined reasoning.

This Constitution exists to preserve that purpose.

Technologies will evolve.

Architectures will change.

Contributors will come and go.

The principles expressed here are intended to provide continuity across those changes.

Every contributor accepts stewardship of these principles.

---

# Article I — Purpose

Atlas exists to improve the quality of investment decisions through evidence, transparency, and disciplined reasoning.

Atlas shall never exist to maximize trading activity, user engagement, or speculative behavior.

Every capability introduced into Atlas shall demonstrably support its purpose.

---

# Article II — Truth

Facts shall remain distinct from interpretation.

Interpretation shall remain distinct from opinion.

Opinion shall remain distinct from personal decision.

Atlas shall never knowingly present interpretation as objective fact.

---

# Article III — Explainability

Every meaningful conclusion produced by Atlas shall be explainable.

Users shall be able to understand:

* the evidence considered,
* the assumptions applied,
* the calculations performed,
* and the reasoning that produced the result.

Opaque or unverifiable recommendations are inconsistent with the purpose of Atlas.

---

# Article IV — Evidence

Evidence shall take precedence over preference.

When evidence contradicts an existing model, the model shall be questioned before the evidence is dismissed.

Every significant model should identify its assumptions.

---

# Article V — Privacy

Personal financial information belongs exclusively to its owner.

Atlas shall be designed so that users may operate the system entirely under their own control.

Cloud services may be supported where they provide clear value, but local operation shall remain a first-class capability.

User data shall never become a product.

Privacy is a core design requirement rather than an optional feature.

---

# Article VI — Simplicity

Complexity must justify its existence.

Every feature shall improve decision quality sufficiently to warrant its maintenance cost.

Whenever two approaches satisfy the same objective, Atlas shall favor the simpler design.

---

# Article VII — Stewardship

Every contributor is a steward of Atlas.

Stewardship includes:

* preserving architectural clarity,
* documenting significant decisions,
* improving maintainability,
* respecting prior work,
* and leaving the project in a better state than it was found.

---

# Article VIII — Independence

Atlas shall remain independent of commercial influence that compromises objective analysis.

Recommendations, rankings, and research shall never be altered to benefit advertisers, sponsors, referral programs, or other financial incentives.

The interests of the investor shall remain paramount.

---

# Article IX — Intellectual Honesty

Atlas shall acknowledge uncertainty where uncertainty exists.

Confidence shall never be represented as certainty.

Models shall communicate their limitations.

The absence of sufficient evidence shall be treated as meaningful information rather than concealed by artificial precision.

---

# Article X — Documentation

Significant architectural decisions shall be documented.

Project philosophy shall remain visible.

Institutional knowledge shall not depend upon individual memory.

Documentation is part of the product.

---

# Article XI — Longevity

Atlas shall favor maintainability over expediency.

Architectural decisions should consider the long-term health of the project rather than short-term convenience.

Backward compatibility should be preserved whenever practical.

Breaking changes require deliberate justification.

---

# Article XII — Governance

The Foundation Documents collectively define the identity of Atlas.

Architectural Decision Records explain why significant technical decisions were made.

Implementation follows architecture.

Architecture follows principles.

Principles follow this Constitution.

When conflict exists, this Constitution prevails.

---

# Article XIII — The Investor

Atlas serves the investor.

The investor does not serve Atlas.

Atlas informs.

Atlas explains.

Atlas measures.

Atlas documents.

But Atlas cannot define purpose.

Purpose belongs to the investor.

The investor alone possesses the responsibility to determine personal goals, acceptable risk, time horizon, values, priorities, and the future they wish to build.

Atlas exists to help the investor make decisions that remain faithful to those intentions.

The final investment decision shall always remain a human responsibility.

---

# Article XIV — Amendment

The Constitution is intended to remain stable.

Amendments shall be rare.

Any amendment should satisfy all of the following:

* address a demonstrated need,
* preserve the spirit of Atlas,
* improve long-term clarity,
* and be documented through an Architectural Decision Record explaining the reasoning for the change.

The Constitution shall evolve deliberately rather than incrementally.

---

# Closing Declaration

The strength of Atlas will never be measured solely by its software.

Its enduring value will be measured by the consistency with which it applies these principles across changing technologies, changing markets, and changing generations of contributors.

Every contributor is entrusted with preserving that consistency.

The Constitution is therefore not merely a description of Atlas.

It is the standard to which Atlas is held.
