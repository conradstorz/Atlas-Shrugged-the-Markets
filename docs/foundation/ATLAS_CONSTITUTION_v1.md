# Atlas Constitution v1.0

Atlas exists to help a long-term investor make better decisions under uncertainty.
It does not predict markets, sell advice, or replace human judgment.

## Article I — Maps, Not Predictions
Atlas shall organize evidence, assumptions, risks, and decisions. It shall not pretend to know the future.

## Article II — Privacy by Default
Personal financial information belongs to the investor. Atlas shall run on private infrastructure, store private data locally by default, and avoid telemetry.

## Article III — Facts, Models, Beliefs, and Decisions Are Separate
Atlas shall distinguish objective facts from analytical models, investor beliefs, and investor decisions.

## Article IV — Every Score Is Explainable
Every score shall be decomposable into components, weights, raw inputs, and rationale.

## Article V — Every Holding Has a Purpose
Atlas shall encourage every position to have a written thesis: why it deserves capital, what role it plays, and what evidence would change the investor's mind.

## Article VI — Reduce Avoidable Mistakes
Atlas is not built to maximize excitement. It is built to reduce avoidable errors: hidden concentration, needless overlap, emotional decisions, unmanaged risk, and forgotten assumptions.

## Article VII — YAGNI With Foresight
Atlas shall avoid speculative features, but preserve foundational architecture that becomes expensive to change later: data model, package boundaries, explainable scoring, and provider interfaces.

## Article VIII — Evidence Before Opinion
Opinions are allowed only when labeled as opinions and tied to evidence or explicit assumptions.

## Article IX — The Decision Journal Is Sacred
Past decisions shall be preserved with their original reasoning so outcomes can be judged honestly.

## Article X — Local First, Portable Always
Atlas shall be easy to run locally, easy to back up, and portable across private servers.
